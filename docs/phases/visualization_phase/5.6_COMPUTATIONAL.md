## 3. Per-Claim Specification

### C3.1 — Speedup Factor

**Population** (single-column): Horizontal bar chart, log x-axis. Speedup = median(T_GED) / median(T_IsalGraph). Two bars per dataset: exhaustive and greedy pipelines. Vertical line at speedup = 1 (breakeven). Annotate geometric mean speedup. Error bars: IQR-derived.

**Individual** (single-column): Select one representative pair from LINUX. Horizontal timeline: top bar = GED time (red), bottom bar = stacked encode_i + encode_j + Lev time (blue/green/purple segments). Annotate "Speedup = X.Xx".

**Table**: Columns: Dataset, T_GED (ms) [med ± IQR], T_encode (ms), T_Lev (ms), T_total (ms), Speedup.

### C3.2 — Crossover Point

**Population** (full width): Log-log plot. X = n (nodes), Y = time per pair. Three curves: GED (A*), IsalGraph(exh), IsalGraph(greedy). Fitted regression lines (dashed). Vertical dashed lines at n*_exh and n*_greedy (crossover). Shaded regions "GED faster" / "IsalGraph faster". Reference complexity lines O(n²), O(n³), O(2ⁿ).

**Individual** (single-column): Zoomed-in view near crossing. Show actual data points + crossing annotation.

**Table**: Columns: Method, Scaling model, Exponent α, R², n* (crossover), Speedup at n=12.

### C3.3 — Amortized Pipeline

**Population** (full width): Stacked bar per dataset. IsalGraph: blue (encoding) + green (Levenshtein). Side-by-side: red (GED total). Y-axis: log-scale total time. Speedup ratio annotated.

**Individual** (single-column): For largest dataset, pie chart decomposing IsalGraph time into encoding % and Levenshtein %. Adjacent single-color pie for GED (100%). Pies sized proportional to absolute time.

**Table**: Columns: Dataset, N, Pairs, T_encode, T_Lev, T_IsalGraph, T_GED, Speedup.

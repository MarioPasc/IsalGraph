## 3. Per-Hypothesis Specification

### H2.1 — Low Distortion (Stress-1 < 0.20 at 2D, < 0.10 at 5D)

**Population** (single-column): X = dimension d ∈ {2,3,5,10}. Y = Stress-1. One line per dataset, solid = Lev(exh), dashed = GED. Threshold lines at 0.20, 0.10, 0.05 with Kruskal interpretation labels.

**Individual** (full width, 1×2): Left = 2D MDS of Levenshtein for IAM LOW, colored by 9 class labels (Paul Tol muted palette). Right = same for GED. This is the advisor's core visual: "grafos parecidos se representan mediante cadenas parecidas."

**Table**: Per dataset×method. Columns: NEV ratio, Stress-1 (2D/5D), Variance explained 2D.

### H2.2 — Geometric Agreement (Procrustes m² < random)

**Population** (single-column): Grouped bar of m² at d=2 and d=5 per dataset. Reference line = median null m² from permutation. Significance stars.

**Individual** (single-column): Superimpose MDS(Lev, 2D) and MDS(GED, 2D) for IAM LOW after Procrustes alignment. Lev = circles (blue), GED = triangles (red). Lines connecting corresponding points, colored by residual magnitude.

**Table**: Per dataset×method×dim. Columns: m², p-value.

### H2.3 — Shepard Fidelity

**Population** (single-column): Grouped bar of Shepard R² at d=2 and d=5 for Lev and GED.

**Individual** (full width, 2×2): Best dataset × {Lev, GED} and worst dataset × {Lev, GED}. Hexbin Shepard diagrams with isotonic regression line.

**Table**: Per dataset×source×dim. Columns: R² (Pearson²), ρ² (Spearman²).
